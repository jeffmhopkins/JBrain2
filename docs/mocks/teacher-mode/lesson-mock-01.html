misconception remediation ⭐
◆Misconception: reading correlation as causation

A strong correlation feels like proof — but you're missing a lurking third variable. Ask: what else climbs in the summer?

☀ Hot weather
drives ice-cream sales up
drives swimming up → drownings up

Ice cream and drownings never touch each other — the heat moves both. That's a confounder.

Think of it this way: roosters crow right before sunrise, every single day.

Perfect correlation — but no one thinks the rooster causes the sun to rise. Something else (the Earth turning) drives both the crowing and the dawn. Same shape as the ice cream.
