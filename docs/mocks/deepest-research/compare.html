How they work

Solid-state cells replace the liquid electrolyte with a solid ion conductor — sulfide, oxide, or polymer — enabling a lithium-metal anode and higher energy density12. The main hurdles are dendrite suppression at the interface and manufacturability at scale7.

Where mass production stands

Pilot lines exist (QuantumScape, Toyota-Idemitsu, Samsung SDI) but yields remain the gating factor; credible mass-market volume is 2027–2030, not sooner14.
