Why line-granularity for math: a LaTeX formula is only renderable once its closing delimiter arrives. Revealing character-by-character means the reader watches $$E_k = \frac{p^2}{ sit there as raw markup (or a placeholder) until it snaps to typeset. Committing on a completed line / closed $$ block means each equation appears once, fully typeset, with a gentle directional fade — no flicker, no reflow.

Self-contained mock — not wired into the app. If KaTeX can't load (offline), math shows as amber raw-LaTeX so the reveal cadence is still visible.
